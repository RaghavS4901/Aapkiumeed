VERIFIED WEBSITES TO CHECK ONLINE

NEW DELHI

People living in Delhi can check for the online availability of vacant beds online by going to the Delhi government website. Users can also log in to https://coronabeds.jantasamvad.org and then click on the bed that they want accordingly.The website shows a list of hospitals along with a total number of beds in that particular hospital along with vacant beds, availability of oxygen beds, ICU, and ventilators. The website also shows the address along with the contact details of the hospital. To make it easier, the website has marked the hospitals as Red, Green, and Yellow. Red means that there are no beds available, while Yellow indicates that there are less than 50 beds available. Furthermore, the Green colour indicates that there are 50 or more beds available in the hospital.

GURGAON

Gurgaon authorities have introduced a dedicated portal to check the availability of beds for COVID patients. Users can go to COVIDGGN.COMand can search for dedicated COVID-19 hospitals for an emergency. Furthermore, the website also real-time count of active cases, recovered cases, deceased cases, and confirmed cases. It also displays the fixed rate of different tests including CBNAAT, TrueNat, RT-PCR, rapid antigen, IgG based ELISA testing, and more. To get the details of COVID-19 hospital, users can go to the hamburger menu and select the Hospital tab. The list shows, the hospital name, number of available oxygen beds, ICU beds, ventilator beds, contact number of the hospital, and address.

THANE

People who are living in the Thane region can also check for the availability of beds online if they are in an emergency. One can go to https://covidthane.org website and check for the availability of vacant beds for COVID patients. The website shows a detailed report about the total capacity, number of beds occupied, number of vacant beds, and number of ICU beds available in a hospital. It also shows whether the hospital has oxygen and ventilators in case of an emergency. The portal also gives the location and contact details of the hospital.

BENGALURU

you can go to https://covidbengaluru.com/ website to check for the availability of beds. The website gives you a list of hospitals that have ICU beds, oxygen beds, non-oxygen beds, ventilators, and more. One can also get details of plasma availability by going to the website.

ANDHRA PRADESH

Andhra Pradesh government has also rolled out a real-time dashboard that keeps a track of all the vacant beds available in the state including cities like Vijayawada, Guntur, Vizag, and more. You can go to http://dashboard.covid19.ap.gov.in/ to check the availability of beds. The portal offers a district-wise classification of hospitals. So, for example, one can click on Chittoor and the website will show the list of hospitals in that district. Furthermore, the portal also gives key information about hospital contact numbers, number of ICU beds, number of oxygen beds, number of general beds, and more.

TELANGANA

The Telangana government has also set up a dedicated portal through which you can the details of the dedicated COVID-19 hospitals in cities like Hyderabad, Warangal, Karimnagar, Nizamabad and more. One can visit http://164.100.112.24/SpringMVC to check the availability of beds in their region. The portal offers the full data of government as well as private hospitals in the state. One can click on Govt or private hospitals to check the full list. Furthermore, the portal also shows the list of hospitals, the number of regular beds, oxygen beds, ICU beds, ventilators and more for each district.

WEST BENGAL

West Bengal government has also set up a dedicated portal to check the availability of online beds for COVID patients. The portal offers the status of vacant beds, number of ICU beds, general beds, and more in several cities including Kolkata and are updated on a daily basis. One can click on the present date and check the availability of vacant beds in different districts. The page also offers the district, name of the private hospital, number of beds, and vacant beds. You can visit this website: https://www.wbhealth.gov.in/pages

PUNE

There is a dedicated website to track all the availability of beds in Pune. One can go to https://www.divcommpunecovid.com/ and check for the availability of beds online. Furthermore, the website also flashes emergency contacts like ambulance, PMC control Room, and more. The portal has a filter that allows you to search hospital near your locality.
One can select Pune as a district, select the area, and hospital name to check the availability of beds near your area. Furthermore, the website also shows the status of hospitals in Kolhapur, Sangli, Satara, and Solapur. The portal shows all the details of the hospital including address, numbers, official name and designation, charges, the total number of beds, occupied beds, vacant beds, and more.
There is another website known as covidpune.comThe website gives you all the information about the availability of beds at your nearby hospitals. Individuals can also get information about plasma availability, rapid antigen test centres, ambulance details, Remdesivir information, and tiffin/food service on the website.

AHMEDABAD

People staying in Ahmedabad can check for the availability of beds online by going to https://ahna.org.in/covid19.html website. The portal is maintained by Ahmedabad Hospitals and Nursing Homes Association and it provides some helpful information. The website shows a list of hospitals, which are updated on a daily basis along with key details availability of beds, the total number of beds, and more. The portal also shows whether the hospital has oxygen beds, ICUs and ventilator support or not.
